Moringa

In Senegal, 26 percent of the population is undernourished and chronic malnutrition levels are particularly high among children under five. Improved nutrition involves better access to healthy food and intentionally utilizing what's available. Volunteers work toward sustainable nutrition development through education, diet adjustment, and organized community ation plans.

Diet

In addition to producing more food, improved diet involves better utilization of the nutritious food already available. PCVs promote a number of fortified foods that call upon local plants and resources.

Moringa Olifera is a tree whose leaves are lauded for high vitamin and mineral content. Known locally as "nebedie," the tree thrives even in harsh environmental conditions and relies on little water. Volunteers promote planting moringa in intensive beds, as alleycropping, and in gardens or family compounds.

The leaves pack a lot of nutrition either cooked or dried and pounded into a powder supplement. For a child aged 1-3, a 100g serving of fresh cooked moringa leaves provides all of her daily requirement of calcium, 75 percent of her iron, half her protein, all essential amino acids, and important amounts of potassium, B vitamins, copper. Just 20g provide a child with all of the vitamins A and C she needs.
