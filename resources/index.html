Highlighted Resources

Peace Corps Senegal volunteers have created a wide variety of helpful resources for current and future volunteers in order to pass on their experience and expertise. These comprehensive guides are part of our broader strategy to retain institutional memory within Peace Corps Senegal. Volunteers over the years have been able to create new guides and add to existing ones. Here is a selection of resources created by Peace Corps Volunteers in coordination with local counterparts. We also have a new Resource Library, containing all of our currently hosted media and case studies.

The new Dakar Guide is now available.

Agroforestry Workshop Course Catalog
This is a list of workshop lessons that are available pertaining to agroforestry. These sessions are open to anyone who would like to learn. Like the language workshops, we would ask that there be at least three people interested in the lesson, and pick at least three of the lessons as to make at least a full day.


Live Fencing Pocket Manual

The live fencing manual is an in-depth explanation of the principles and application of live fencing. This manual includes photos, diagrams, sample data, a case study, and a listing of preferred trees for use in the field. Designed for use by non-Agfos, this manual is a must for anyone working with live fencing.


Tree Identification Manuel

This is a full color photo tree identification manual for tree species we can find here in Senegal and west africa. Original photos came from the Trees and Shrubs of the African Sahel manual and have been compiled into just pictures with scientific names.


Agroforestry Manual

PC/Senegal Agroforestry volunteers revised and updated the PC/The Gambia Agroforestry Manual to contain new information and photos pertaining specifically to Senegal.


Conservation Tillage: Methods and Background

An excellent document about conservation tillage compiled by RPCV Whitney Brim-DeForest in coordination with agriculture APCD Famara Massaly. Covers techniques for halting soil erosion, effectively utilizing mulching, and other methods of mainting healthy soil.


Field Crop Manual: Pocket Handbook

A pocket handbook for field crops created by RPCV Whitney Brim-DeForest in coordination with agriculture APCD Famara Massaly. Created to help new volunteers orient themselves to the different varieties of crops of Senegal.


Common Agricultural Pests and Diseases: Identification Guide

An excellent guide to the pests of Senegal compiled by PCV Peter Treut. Includes full color photos of pests and the damage they leave to plants. Also includes strategies for minimizing pest damage, including non-toxic pesticides.


Health Kit

A guide for Health Volunteers compiled by Health PCV's. Includes strategies for classroom and community teaching that have been developed by volunteers through the years. The Health Kit covers the top 10 illnesses/health issues affecting Senegal today.


Senegad Activity Guide

A how-to guide designed to encourage Peace Corps Volunteers to include Gender and Development activities in their primary sector work at site.


Senegal Cross Culture Journal

An excellent journal developed by staff and volunteers to help incoming volunteers better understand Senegalese culture and the importance of cross-cultural exchange.


Senegal "Creating a Mural" Guide

Created by Peace Corps Senegal Volunteers, this manual gives step-by-step instructions for creating a mural and many templates for educational murals.


Beekeeping Guide

This guide, created by Peace Corps the Gambia, gives detailed instructions for building bee hives and harvesting, processing, & utilizing hive products.


Moringa Pocket Guide

A short booklet created by Peace Corps Senegal that describes basic propagation methods and uses for Moringa oleifera, one of the trees most commonly planted by volunteers in Senegal.


Naka Lanuy Jëmbëte Garab?

An illustrated manual in Wolof about basic tree propagation, created by Peace Corps Senegal Agroforestry Volunteers in 2009.
