Media


Peace Corps Senegal has been working to extend techniques and skills to our counterparts using multi-media tools such as instructional video podcasts, local-language radio shows, and documentary films. Click on the links before to learn more about how we're working in each of these areas and download some of the resources we've created.

How To

Peace Corps Senegal staff and volunteers are creating short, clear, instructional videos about specific skills that are easily replicable. They are posted on our iTunes podcast and available for download directly from our site. Currently, the videos are available in English and are being used to solidify PCVs' knowledge months after training. We have already started the process of translating them into local languages so they can be used as a tool for both volunteers and host-country counterparts.

Radio

Peace Corps Senegal Volunteers are expanding their reach beyond their host communities through the use of the airwaves. Local language radio is the primary means of mass communication in rural Senegal, making it an effective tool for community development.

Video

A variety of videos have been produced by Peace Corps Senegal volunteers.
Some succesful projects have had video features produced about them. Visit our Videos page to learn more about our Joal Waste Management project or our Dindefello Summer Camp.
Peace Corps Volunteers have also produced videos to help in large-group education. Elle Travaille, Elle Vie is documentary about successful Senegalese women and is often screened at girls' youth clubs and leadership camps hosted by PC/Senegal volunteers. Pepinieres du Sahel is an 18-minute, French-language video about how to construct a tree nursery.
